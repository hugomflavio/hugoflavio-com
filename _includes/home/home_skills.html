Course teaching

I've taught bachelor-level courses in ecophysiology, highlighting the role and importance of comparative physiology in advancing our knowledge of the natural world. Students are encouraged to attentively observe their surroundings, and in-class debates foment and healthy exchanging of ideas and viepoints.

I've also led several study groups focused on R coding and statistical analyses, and past workshop topics include biomonitoring of rivers, acoustic telemetry data analysis, and R coding in general.

Aquatic animal husbandry

Recirculating Aquatic Systems (RAS) are a great way to maintain aquatic organisms. I have been trained in the different life-support systems for aquatic animals, and can confidently build RAS. This also extends to the gradient that goes from fully flow-through systems to fully closed systems.

Experimental design

Both for my own experiments and when mentoring students, experimental design plays a central role in how I prepare my research. From the numbers of animals required, to the groups we need and the variables we aim to collect, everything is thought through before the experiment starts. This, together with a previously-drawn timeline, ensures that no time nor animals are wasted when we do research.

Team leadership

Over the years, I have learned how to work with a wide variety of personalities. I strive to be a present colleague and mentor. I make a strong effort to get to know those working with me and understand what matters to them. I aim to make sure our work environment feels safe and welcoming for everyone. I want students to feel that their ideas are welcome, and that we are working together to achieve a common goal. Ultimately, I want science to be challenging but fun for everyone involved :)
